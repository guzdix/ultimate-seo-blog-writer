## Dash and Hyphen Control

Avoid using hyphens, en dashes, and em dashes unless they are grammatically required.

Do not use:
- Em dash: —
- En dash: –
- Hyphen as decorative punctuation: -
- Hyphen to create unnecessary compound words

Never write phrases like:
- AI-powered
- SEO-friendly
- long-form
- source-backed
- user-focused
- high-quality
- step-by-step
- copy-paste
- rank-ready
- conversion-driven

Rewrite them naturally instead:
- AI powered content → content powered by AI
- SEO friendly article → article optimized for SEO
- long form blog post → detailed blog post
- source backed claims → claims supported by sources
- user focused writing → writing focused on users
- high quality content → quality content
- step by step guide → complete guide with clear steps
- copy paste ready → ready to copy and paste
- rank ready article → article prepared for ranking
- conversion driven CTA → CTA designed for conversions

Hyphens are allowed only when they are truly required, such as:
- official brand names
- URLs
- code
- file names
- schema values
- dates
- negative numbers
- technical terms that are commonly written with hyphens
- quoted source titles that already contain hyphens

Before delivering any final content, scan the output and remove unnecessary hyphens, en dashes, and em dashes.

If a hyphenated phrase can be rewritten without changing the meaning, rewrite it.

---

## Dash Quality Check

Before final delivery, verify:

- No em dash is used anywhere.
- No en dash is used anywhere.
- No decorative hyphen is used as punctuation.
- No unnecessary hyphenated compound words are used.
- Hyphens appear only in URLs, file names, code, dates, schema, brand names, or terms where the hyphen is truly required.

---

## Strict Output Rule

If the final article contains unnecessary hyphens, en dashes, or em dashes, revise the article before showing it to the user.

The final output must prioritize natural phrasing without dash punctuation.
